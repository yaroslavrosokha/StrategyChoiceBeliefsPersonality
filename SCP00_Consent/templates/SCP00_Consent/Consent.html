Key Information: Please take time to review this information carefully. This is a research study. Your participation in this study is voluntary which means that you may choose not to participate at any time without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled. You may ask questions to the researchers about the study whenever you would like. If you decide to take part in the study be sure you understand what you will do and any possible risks or benefits.

What is the purpose of this study? This is research to learn how people make economic decisions using human subjects as participants.

What will I do if I choose to be in this study? The experiment will begin with a set of questionnaires presented to you on the computer screen. After the questionnaires, you will be given instructions for the experiment. After the instructions, the experiment will begin. In the experiment, you will face a sequence of matches against randomly selected participants in this room. In each match, you will make decisions. For each decision, you will choose one of the available options. At the end of the experiment, you will be asked to complete a feedback questionnaire. The data collected in the experiment will include the choices you have made, as well as your responses to questionnaires. The data will recorded using randomly assigned identification numbers that are not linked to your identity.

How long will I be in the study? The entire experiment (including instructions) is expected to last less than 90 minutes.

What are the possible risks or discomforts? Risks are minimal and are no greater than those encountered in everyday activities. Because the experiment is in a group, other subjects will know that you participated, but they will not learn the choices you make. In addition, we will take precautions to minimize the risk of breach of confidentiality as described in the confidentiality section.

Are there any potential benefits? There is no direct benefit to you.

What alternatives are available? You may choose not to participate in this research study.

Will I receive payment or other incentive? You will be paid in cash at the end of the experiment. The amount of money earned depends upon your decisions and on chance. The typical amount ranges between $5 and $25 per person. You are guaranteed to receive $5 regardless of your decisions. You will receive $5 even if you choose to withdraw from the experiment. According to the rules of the Internal Revenue Service (IRS), payments that are made to you as a result of your participation in a study may be considered taxable income.

Will information about me and my participation be kept confidential? The project's research records may be reviewed by the US DHHS Office for Human Research Protections, and by departments at [...university...] responsible for regulatory and research oversight. Data from the experiment are recorded using randomly assigned identification numbers that are not linked to your identity. The data will be stored indefinitely on the researcherâ€™s computer. The data may be used in future research and shared with other researchers. You will need to sign a receipt in order to get paid. The receipt with your name and signature may be provided to the business office in order to facilitate reimbursement.

What are my rights if I take part in this study? Your participation in this study is voluntary. You may choose not to participate or, if you agree to participate, you can withdraw your participation at any time without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled.
